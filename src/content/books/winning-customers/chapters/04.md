# B2B Commerce, Wholesale & Distribution

## Key Ideas

- B2B buyers now use ten channels per journey and want the "rule of thirds": one-third in-person, one-third remote, one-third self-service. Hybrid selling generates up to 50% more revenue.
- Selling breadth correlates with share growth: 72% of companies selling through seven or more channels grew share.
- The distributor playbook: migrate reordering to self-service, embed into customer operations (vending, VMI, EDI), redeploy reps to advisory work — Grainger, Fastenal, and Sysco all disclose the numbers.
- Marketplace monetisation is a strategic choice: Faire's 15% take rate versus IndiaMART's freemium subscriptions versus lead-gen.
- ABM plus intent data concentrates effort on in-market accounts — one manufacturer built a $181M pipeline in a single quarter.

B2B buying has changed more in five years than in the previous fifty, and the data comes largely from primary sources: McKinsey's B2B Pulse survey of nearly 4,000 decision-makers across 34 sectors and 13 countries, Gartner buyer surveys, Forrester economic-impact studies, and the investor disclosures of the large distributors themselves.

## The omnichannel B2B buyer and the rule of thirds

B2B customers now use an average of ten interaction channels in a single buying journey — double the five they used in 2016. Crucially, they want a balanced mix: at any stage of the journey, roughly one-third prefer in-person interaction, one-third remote by phone or video, and one-third digital self-service. McKinsey calls this the "rule of thirds."

E-commerce has become the single largest B2B revenue channel among companies that offer it, contributing more than a third of revenue, while in-person's share fell five points in a year. 32% of buyers now rank e-commerce their single most effective channel, ahead of in-person at 23%.

Two findings should reshape go-to-market design. First, selling breadth correlates with share: 72% of B2B companies that sell through seven or more channels grew market share, and building one's own marketplace was associated with a 1.7 times higher incidence of share growth. Second, digital is no longer only for small orders — one in five B2B decision-makers will spend between $500,000 and $5M in a single remote or self-service interaction, and 7% will transact more than $5M fully online.

## The rep-free paradox

Gartner adds a sharp nuance. The headline is that 67% of B2B buyers now say they prefer a "rep-free" experience. But the same research shows buyers still prefer hybrid engagement for most tasks: on average they want to complete three buying activities both online and with a rep, 2.3 with a rep alone, and only 1.8 via pure self-service.

The resolution is that self-service must augment reps, not replace them — buyers want to control the process and pull in human help on their own terms. Hybrid selling models, which do exactly this, generate up to 50% more revenue than traditional ones.

## Distributor and wholesaler digital transformation

The industrial distributors offer the clearest, best-documented playbook because they disclose digital penetration in investor materials. Their common pattern is to run field sales and digital self-service as one integrated model, and to embed themselves in the customer's operations through vendor-managed inventory and system integration.

- Grainger's High-Touch Solutions business generates more than 75% of revenue through digital channels while still fielding over 4,000 sales representatives — the hybrid model in a single P&L. Its lower-touch Endless Assortment segment — Zoro in the US, MonotaRO in Japan — runs a web-only long-tail catalogue of some 38 million SKUs serving 16 million-plus registered users.
- Fastenal has pushed its "Digital Footprint" to 62% of sales and targets 66%, largely by physically installing vending machines and bin-stock devices in customer facilities and integrating EDI ordering — turning distribution into embedded infrastructure.
- Sysco routes 80% of orders through its Sysco Shop platform and attributes $450M of incremental sales to an AI personalization engine, with more than $1B projected at maturity.
- Ferguson runs distribution centres, branches, counters, specialist sales, showrooms, and e-commerce as one integrated "common network."

The transferable mechanisms are consistent: migrate transactional reordering to self-service, integrate directly into customer systems through EDI, punch-out, and vendor-managed inventory, and redeploy field sellers onto higher-value advisory work.

## B2B marketplaces and platform economics

Platforms acquire two sides at once and monetise the transaction between them, but their models vary widely. Faire, the wholesale marketplace connecting independent brands and retailers, charges a 15% take rate on standard orders plus a $10 new-customer fee to brands, and uses that revenue to fund the acquisition hooks that grow the retailer side — net-60 payment terms, free returns on first orders, and working capital. Amazon Business has scaled to more than $35B in annualised gross sales by extending Amazon's consumer marketplace mechanics to procurement.

Not all platforms take a cut of transactions. IndiaMART, India's largest B2B marketplace, runs a freemium supplier-subscription model: of roughly 8.7 million supplier storefronts, only about 220,000 — under 3% — pay, at an average of about ₹66,000 a year, while the other 97%-plus are served free to maximise buyer-side liquidity. The strategic spread is wide: vertical software typically monetises at under a 1% take rate, whereas marketplaces commonly take 20% or more. The choice of monetisation model — transaction take-rate versus subscription versus lead-generation — is itself a core growth-strategy decision.

## Acquisition tactics: ABM and intent data

On the demand-generation side, account-based marketing paired with intent data has produced some of the best-documented B2B outcomes. A global industrial fastening manufacturer using the 6sense intent platform generated $181M in pipeline and $9.7M in closed-won revenue in a single quarter by identifying and prioritising in-market accounts before they raised their hands.

At the smaller end, an integrated website-plus-SEO-plus-paid-search program lifted website-generated revenue 212% in the first 90 days for an industrial rope-access training firm. The mechanism is the same at both scales: concentrate marketing and sales effort on the accounts that intent signals show are already in-market.

## Retention, expansion, and manufacturers going direct

In B2B, retention is expressed as net revenue retention, and its financial power is stark: top-quartile-valued B2B software companies reach 113% NRR — meaning they grow 13% a year without winning any new business — versus 98% for the bottom quartile. Expansion is engineered through customer-success programs, volume rebates, replenishment and subscription models, configure-price-quote tooling, and personalised pricing.

Manufacturers and wholesalers are also going direct through digital commerce, with measurable results. A Forrester Total Economic Impact study of Salesforce B2B Commerce modelled a composite $1B manufacturer achieving 289% ROI and a payback of under six months, as self-service adoption grew from 12% to 47% of accounts over three years and average order values rose up to 25%.

Named cases echo the pattern: Steelcase now drives 90% of its B2B sales through online channels built on a multi-site commerce platform with punch-out e-procurement integration, and wholesaler AMR Hair & Beauty, after moving to a self-service B2B storefront in 2023, saw average order value rise 77%, conversion grow 93% year over year, and sales triple. The through-line: giving business buyers a genuine self-service channel does not cannibalise sales — it expands them, provided human sellers remain available for complex work.

## In Practice

- Design go-to-market around the rule of thirds: every stage of the journey needs an in-person, remote, and self-service path.
- Let self-service augment reps, never replace them — buyers want control plus on-demand human help.
- Steal the distributor playbook: self-service for reordering, system integration for lock-in, reps for advisory value.
- If you run a platform, choose your monetisation model deliberately — take-rate, subscription, or lead-gen — based on which side needs liquidity.
- Prioritise accounts by intent signals; concentrate spend where the data says buyers are already in-market.
